    Ahmed Osman

    Graduated from Art Education College, Helwan University, completed a Diploma in Computer Graphic and Web Design from Herzing College, Canada and he obtained the Master Degree of Art. Currently he is teaching digital design at

    Sur College of Applied Sciences.

    He participated in several sculpture, painting and graphic design exhibitions. He is interested in creating abstract, land art and Mandala, his art inspired from Ancient Arts, nature and emotional experiments, he likes to analyze art work, believing in art as a reflection tool to our souls.

    Ahmed designed a number of websites for organizations and individuals in Canada and Egypt, also he is an active member in both Plastic International Association of Art (UNESCO) and Design Exchange Association (Canada).

  • Research

    “Mandala's Drawings and its Effectiveness Role to Reduce Illegitimate Pre-School Children's Aggressiveness”

     

    Abstract:

    The study aimed to provide the effectiveness of using Mandala Drawings in reducing the aggressiveness behavior to specimen of Illegitimate pre-school children.

    To prove it, the researcher showed the basic items of the research and the related studies of every item.

    The researcher also did experiment on specimen of illegitimate children with aggressive behavior. He made a pre-post application to measure the aggressiveness to see the differences.

    He noticed that "according to the marks of study cases on the measurements of the Pre-Post aggressive behavior" there are statically significance differences for the Post application.
